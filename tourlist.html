    Korean Buddhism Zen Retreat 12 Days Tour 11 Nights

    Experience Korea’s Buddhist culture through a 12-day, 11-night Korean Buddhist Zen tour! Starting from Jogyesa Temple, temple life programs are held at Bongeunsa Temple, Naksansa Temple, etc., allowing you to fully experience Korea's Buddhist culture. Program contents include temple tours, tea ceremonies, and meditation. You can also experience mask dancing at Andong Hahoe Village, and in Busan, you can visit Haeundae Beach and Jagalchi Market.

    Soul Of Korea - 9 Days 8 Nights Tour

    I highly recommend exploring tourism to those who want to fully experience Korea's history and culture. Along with the Blue House Sarangchae, which can be said to be the center of Korea, Gyeongbokgung Palace, the most famous palace in Seoul, and a DMZ tour where you can feel the painful history of Korea will be held. Additionally, we will visit Andong Hahoe Village in Gyeongju and explore Anapji Pond and Seokguram Grotto. On the last day, you can experience a unique and fun underwater exploration in Jeju.

    Korean Cultural Experience Tour

    Korea's beautiful culture comes to life in this package itinerary. Train your body and mind by taking Taekwondo classes while feeling the atmosphere of Gyeongbokgung Palace, Jogyesa Temple, and Zen Buddhism. After that, you can have the experience of making and eating kimchi, which can be said to be Korea's representative food. And experience t he Korean hanbok worn during the Joseon Dynasty. Along with that, visit Namsangol Hanok Village and feel the Hanok culture.

  • 04

    Unesco Korea Bucket List Tour 8 Days 7 Nights

    This tour has a special meaning because it consists only of UNESCO World Heritage sites. First, you will visit Hwaseong, a representative heritage site located in Suwon. Then, head to Gyeongju to visit Seokguram Grotto, one of the best Buddhist shrines in Asia, surrounded by Bodhisattvas and guardian deities, and Andong Hahoe Village, where Seonyu Julbul Nori is preserved. Afterwards, explore Jongmyo Shrine, where you can experience Korean Confucian culture along with Changdeokgung Palace in Seoul.

    Hi! Seoul 3 Days 2 Nights Tour

    You can experience the culture of Seoul, Korea's representative city, in a detailed and concise manner for 3 days and 2 nights. First, you will visit the Blue House Sarangchae and learn about the history of Seoul, and then visit Gyeongbokgung Palace, the representative palace. Afterwards, experience the Insa-dong Antique Street, which is much loved by foreigners, visit major galleries, and even enjoy free shopping! We will guide you so that you can fully experience the scent of Seoul.

  • 06

    Culinary Arts & Culture Tour - 8days 7nights

    Korean food is nutritious and balanced while being low in calories. Maybe that's why it's gaining a lot of popularity among foreigners. It is widely known as a safe food that anyone can enjoy due to its unique flavor and spicy taste. On this tour, you can enjoy a variety of experiences such as making kimchi and bibimbap, royal cuisine, and tasting bulgogi, wild octopus, and makgeolli. It will be a refreshing experience to try traditional rice cakes while visiting the Rice Cake Museum in Bukchon Hanok Village!

    Touch Of Korea - 5 Days 4 Nights Tour

    In addition to a tour of Seoraksan Mountain, one of the most beautiful mountains in Korea, the filming location of the drama <Winter Sonata>, which attracted a lot of attention in Asia, we plan to visit Gwongeumseong Fortress and Sinheungsa Temple by cable car. You will also experience the Suwon Korean Folk Village, a popular tourist attraction among foreign visitors. We will travel to Seoul with him and have a great time learning about the history of Seoul, the capital of Korea's past presidents, at the Blue House Sarangchae.

  • 08

    Essence Of Korea Tour - 5 Days 4 Nights

    This tour allows you to feel and understand Seoul broadly. Along with visiting the Blue House Sarangchae, we plan to experience Seoul's magnificent Gyeongbokgung Palace and Jogyesa Temple, the best temple where Korean Buddhism counts votes. We will also visit the UNESCO-designated Suwon Hwaseong Fortress and the Korean Folk Village, where you can see the Korean lifestyle. And on the last day, we will head to the DMZ, Korea's demilitarized zone, and experience the pain of Korea's division.

  • 09

    World Heritage Tour 7 Days 6 Nights

    This is a meaningful tour recommended for foreigners who want to experience and feel Korea's world heritage. First, we will visit Suwon's iconic Hwaseong Fortress, and then experience Bulguksa Temple, Seokguram Grotto, and Haeinsa Temple, one of Gyeongju's best-known temples. Additionally, we plan to experience Changdeokgung Palace, which can be said to be the epitome of traditional Korean garden design, and the National Folk Museum, where you can study Korea's traditional lifestyle, religious rites, and customs, for 6 nights and 7 days.

  • 10

    Seoul 2n 3d Semi Private Tour Package

    This tour is a semi-private tour in Seoul. After unpacking at the hotel, the city tour begins. Pass the Blue House and visit Gyeongbokgung Palace, the oldest and grandest of Seoul's royal palaces. Gyeongbokgung Palace is a masterpiece of traditional Korean architecture and one of the most visited places in Seoul. Inside is the National Folk Museum of Korea, which displays religious rituals, customs, food, and the social dynamics of traditional Korean life. After that, go to the National Ginseng Store or Amethyst Factory. Afterwards, the tour ends with free time in Seoul.

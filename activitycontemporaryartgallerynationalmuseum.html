Contemporary Art Gallery & National Museum Tour

Contemporary Art Gallery & National Museum Tour

Our first stop was the National Museum of Korea, the largest museum in Korea, housing valuable cultural assets dating back to ancient times. And the Leeum Samsung Museum of Art, designed by world-famous architects Mario Botta, Jean Nouvel, and Rem Koolhaas, presents attractive buildings and works to you. Afterwards, you can see the National Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art, where you can see various classic works of modern art, and finally, feel the Samcheong-dong road that runs from Gyeongbokgung Palace to Samcheong Tunnel.

Duration: 4 Hour(s) - 0 Minute(s)

Tour Category: Gallery&museum

contact us

Package Itinerary

  • National Museum → Leeum Museum → National Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art → Samcheong-dong

  • Start your tour off with a hotel pickup in Seoul. Your first stop would be the National Museum of Korea, which houses precious cultural assets dating back to the ancient days. It is the largest museum in Korea and preserves invaluable Korean cultural assets that silently tell the story of Korea’s fascinating history, from ancient days to the modern era.

  • National Museum

  • Leeum Museum

  • Next, visit Leeum, Samsung Museum of Art. You’ll notice that the building itself is just as fascinating as the art on display since it was designed by the internationally renowned architects Mario Botta, Jean Nouvel, and Rem Koolhaas.

    You will see the traditional Korean calligraphy, paintings, ceramics, and metal crafts on the first level. Head up to the second level to browse exhibits of modern and contemporary art by Korean and international artists.

  • We then continue to the National Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art. You will see classical works of modern art ranging from paintings in Indian Ink to marvelous sculptures. Aside from the exhibitions, the center also hosts musical performances, lectures, workshops, and seminars for artists.

  • National Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art

  • Samcheong-dong

  • At the end of your tour, take a relaxing stroll through the street where traditional and modern Korea combines. The Samcheongdong-gil Road, which stretches from Gyeongbokgung Palace to the Samcheong Tunnel is lined with cafes, museums, antique shops, and art galleries.

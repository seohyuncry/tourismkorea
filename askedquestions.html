A.

If you travel by plane for more than 3 hours, your biorhythm will be disrupted and jet lag will occur. When jet lag occurs, you may feel drowsy during the day, have trouble sleeping at night, feel tired, and have trouble concentrating at your destination.

- How to control the amount of time you are exposed to sunlight
It is based on the fact that when there is sunlight (daytime), the hormone called melatonin secreted from the brain decreases, and when it gets dark (at night), melatonin increases, making you sleepy. It is an attempt to adjust the biological rhythm to the destination by adjusting the time of exposure to sunlight. no see. When traveling east, if the time difference is less than 6 hours, soak in the sun for 2-3 hours in the morning. If the time difference is 7-12 hours, stay indoors in the morning and soak in the sun in the afternoon. When traveling west, on the contrary, if the time difference is less than 6 hours, you can enjoy sunlight in the afternoon, and if the time difference is 7 to 12 hours, you can enjoy sunlight in the morning.

- Drink a lot of water
Water is the best medicine to overcome jet lag.


- Do not drink alcohol, coffee or tea.
When consumed in excess, alcohol stimulates the nervous system and prevents sleep.

- How to take sleeping pills
The drugs used are usually triazolam (Halcion) and lorazepam (Ativan), which have a short half-life. The initial dose should preferably be no more than 1 pill, and should never be taken with alcohol.

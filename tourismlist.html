TOURISM

  • 01

    Hwasung Fortress, Samsung Delight & Unhyungung Palace Tour

    After hotel pickup in Seoul, take a bus to visit Hwaseong, a UNESCO World Heritage site known as Suwon's official fortress. Afterwards, you will visit the Samsung Delight Shop, which AKorea's latest IT and mobile products and various accessories, and allows you to test the products. Then, you will experience the National Museum of Korea, which houses Korea's precious cultural assets, and finally, visit Unhyeongung Palace, which was the home of the young Emperor Gojong of the Joseon Dynasty.

    learn more
  • 02

    Furniture Museum, Unhyungung Palace & Jogyesa Temple Tour

    This Seoul sightseeing tour begins at the Furniture Museum, where you can see antique furniture from the last dynasty that ruled Korea. We will then tour Unhyeongung Palace, which was the home of the young Emperor Gojong of the late Joseon Dynasty. Afterwards, you will visit Jogyesa Temple, which is located in the center of the city and does not have the solemn, traditional atmosphere of other temples located deep in the mountains. We will visit the shopping district of Insa-dong, one of the most popular places for foreigners.

    learn more
  • 03

    Contemporary Art Gallery & National Museum Tour

    The first stop is the National Museum of Korea, which houses valuable Korean cultural assets dating back to ancient times and is known as the largest museum in Korea. Then, you will visit the Samsung Leeum Museum, where the building itself is as fascinating as the works of art on display. Afterwards, we head to the National Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art, where we can admire classic works of modern art. Samcheong-dong Road, which runs from Gyeongbokgung Palace to Samcheong Tunnel, can also be said to be the highlight of this tour.

    learn more
  • 04

    Traditional Open Market Tour

    Experience life in Korea by visiting the country's most popular outdoor market. Visiting the city's busiest market is a great way to absorb Seoul's vibrant spirit and shop for a variety of items such as medicinal herbs, fresh fruit, textiles, and semi-precious stones. As the tour focuses on markets, we plan to only visit the core places in Korea. Visit Gyeongdong Herb Market, one of Korea's largest ginseng markets,and the lively Dongdaemun Market, a fruit wholesale market.

    learn more
  • 05

    Petite France & Nami Island Tour

    After being picked up in the morning from your Seoul hotel, we head to our first destination, Petite France. It is also famous as a photo spot with its colorful buildings surrounded by a valley. You can also visit the museum dedicated to Antoine de Saint- Exupéry and his works. Then, we visit Nami Island and take a tour of the imaginary country ‘Naminara Republic’. In addition, we will take a walk along the road that was the filming location for Winter Sonata. You can also visit Chuncheon Myeongdong and experience the atmosphere of Myeongdong with lunch.

    learn more
  • 06

    Andong Hahoe Folk Village Day Tour

    We will pass through Hahoe Village, a traditional village from the Joseon Dynasty that is registered as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and watch the Bayersingut Talchum, which has been designated as Important Intangible Cultural Heritage No. 69. Mask dance performances were performed to wish the village much happiness and a good harvest. Move to Buyongdae Cliff to watch the Seonyu Julbul fire, a boat ride fireworks show enjoyed by nobles called noblemen. Lastly, see the masks at the Hahoe Mask Museum and learn about the symbolism of each mask and the history of mask dance.

    learn more
  • 07

    Haeinsa Temple Day Tour

    After being picked up from your hotel, you will visit Haeinsa Temple on Mt. Gayasan, one of the Three Jewels Temples in Korea and a significant place symbolizing the Dharma or teachings of Buddha. Haeinsa Temple's storage hall, known as the Tripitaka Koreana Complex, is the repository for the Goryeo Tripitaka Koreana, which were designated as a national treasure of Korea by the Korean government. The Tripitaka Koreana here was created from the 23rd year of Goryeo (1236) in hopes of overcoming the national crisis caused by the Mongol invasion, and it took 16 years to complete.

    learn more
  • 08

    Gyeongju City Tour ( Unesco World Heritage)

    Today you will visit some of the world's most historically important sites from the Silla Dynasty. Starting with Bulguksa Temple, which means 'Temple of Buddha,' experience the stone Buddha statue quietly overlooking the East Sea by exploring Seokguram Grotto. You can learn more about Silla's traditions at the Gyeongju National Museum and explore Anapji Pond, which was used as a resort during the Silla Dynasty. Afterwards, visit Cheomseongdae, the oldest astronomical observatory in Asia, and finally Gobun-ri Park, designated Historic Site No. 175, to learn about the tombs of Silla kings.

    learn more
  • 09

    Mt. Seorak Tour

    This Mt. Seorak tour prepared by Exploring Tourism is a tour where you can experience the diverse scenery of Mt. Seorak. We head to Seoraksan Mountain, the highest mountain in the Taebaeksan Mountains. Next, take the cable car and head to Gwongeumseong Fortress. The name Gwon Geumseong originated from a legend that two generals named Kwon and Kim built the castle to avoid war. And in addition to visiting Sinheungsa Temple on the mountain, guarded by a bronze Buddha statue, try the current seafood at Daepo Port Fish Market.

    learn more
  • 10

    Dmz Full Day Tour

    The theme of this DMZ full-day tour, where you can feel the sad history of Korea's division, is the Demilitarized Zone (DMZ). This tour, which takes about 8 hours, starts with Imjingak, the Unification Bridge, DMZ, DMZ-related movie screening, exhibition hall, the 3rd Tunnel, which was planned by North Korea as a military invasion route, Dora Observatory, Dorasan Station, and from Unification Village to this small rural area. You will witness local life. The last stop is Paju Premium Outlet, where you can enjoy a variety of shopping.

    learn more

korean food

  • Enjoy traditional Korean food!

  • Bibimbap

The taste of mixing five colors of eggplants

Bibimbap

Bibimbap is one of Korea's representative foods. It is healthy and delicious thanks to the combination of various ingredients and unique spicy seasoning. First, bibimbap contains a variety of vegetables and proteins, allowing for balanced nutritional intake. Second, in the process of mixing various ingredients, a variety of flavors unfold in your mouth, making the meal more enjoyable. Third, bibimbap uses red pepper paste, a traditional Korean fermented seasoning, to give it an exotic taste.

  • Bulgogi

Fall in love with the sweet taste

Bulgogi

Bulgogi is one of the most popular beef dishes in Korea. It is made from thinly sliced ​​sirloin or another prime cut of beef (ribs) and is typically seasoned with a mixture of soy sauce, sesame oil, pepper, garlic, onion, ginger, wine, and sugar. Marinate for 2 to 4 hours to enhance flavor and tenderness. Bulgogi is traditionally grilled, but grilling or pan-searing is also common. Whole garlic, sliced onions, and chopped bell peppers are often roasted or cooked at the same time. It is often served to non-Koreans as their first taste of Korean cuisine.

  • Korean Chicken

What brought about the K-food craze

Korean Chicken

Seasoned chicken, made with crispy fried chicken and a generous amount of red pepper paste seasoning, is a popular late-night snack in Korea. Starting with the first seasoned chicken, there are currently hundreds of types of seasoned chicken, and it is said that young people overseas have fallen in love with the charm of Korean-style chicken where they can choose the flavor they want and cannot escape from it.

  • Ramen

Slurp, salty, delicious

Ramen

You can realize the popularity of Korean ramen by watching Korean spicy ramen challenge videos filled with video platforms. As the number of foreigners trying out intense spicy flavors is increasing, the total stock price of food companies that have released spicy ramen has risen significantly. As a representative food of the common people, ramen often appears in Korean dramas and movies, and many foreigners are looking for ramen after seeing scenes in the videos.

  • Tteogbokk-i

Impressive spicy taste

Tteogbokk-i

Tteokbokki is a traditional Korean food made from long rice cakes with soy sauce or red pepper seasoning. It has long been known as a food similar to palace tteokbokki, but in modern times, red products using red pepper paste or red pepper powder are popular. With the emergence of specialty stores with various menus such as Rose Tteokbokki and Spicy Tteokbokki that captivate the taste buds of consumers, it has become easier than ever to taste delicious Tteokbokki.

  • Gimbab

Pack a lot on picnic days

Gimbab

Gimbap is a representative Korean snack and meal replacement food. It is made by adding rice, various vegetables, and sometimes protein ingredients such as eggs or ham, and rolling it in seaweed. The ingredients are mainly carrots, spinach, cucumber, and pickled radish, and rice seasoned with red pepper paste or sesame oil is also used. Cut all the ingredients into appropriate sizes and place them on top of the seaweed, then spread the rice, top with the ingredients, and roll it up.

  • Pork belly

Even foreigners enjoy eating it

Pork belly

Pork belly is ‘the name given because the meat and fat parts overlap three times’, and it is a wide, flat area from the part where the ribs are removed to the abdomen. The red meat and fat form a three-layer membrane, giving it a great flavor and being loved by not only Koreans but also foreigners. It is the most savory of pork parts and can be cooked in a variety of ways, but it is high in fat and calories due to its high fat content.

Traditional Open Market Tour

Traditional Open Market Tour

Experience life in Korea by visiting the country's most popular outdoor market. Visiting the city's busiest market is a great way to absorb Seoul's vibrant spirit and shop for a variety of items such as medicinal herbs, fresh fruit, textiles, and semi-precious stones. As the tour focuses on markets, we plan to only visit the core places in Korea. Visit Gyeongdong Herb Market, one of Korea's largest ginseng markets, and the lively Dongdaemun Market, a fruit wholesale market.

Duration: 4 Hour(s) - 0 Minute(s)

Tour Category: Half Day Tour

contact us

Package Itinerary

  • Gyeongdong Herb Market → Fruit wholesale market → Dongdaemun Textile Market

  • Experience Korean life as you visit South Korea's most popular open markets. A trip to the city's busiest marketplaces is a great way to soak up the vibrant spirit of Seoul and shop for a variety of items such as herbal medicine, fresh fruits, textiles, and semi-precious stones.

    Visit the Gyeongdong Herb Market, which is one of the largest herbal medicine and ginseng markets in South Korea. Supplying 70% of the nation's herbal medicine ingredients, the market also sells agricultural products and fish. The market also has more than 1,000 related shops and oriental medical clinics in the area.

  • Herb

  • Fruit wholesale market

  • Your next stop would be at the oldest and largest wholesale market in Korea for marine and agricultural products. The Garak-dong Agricultural Market, which opened in 1985, is located in the Songpa-gu district of Seoul. The market offers independent sections for fresh fruits, vegetables, meat, and fish in a large area spanning around 543,450 square meters.

  • Lastly, explore the vibrant Dongdaemun Market where you'll find an interesting mix of tradition and modernity. The market became the heart of the Korean textile industry in the 1970s and has further developed to become the center of Korea's wholesale and retail fashion industries. The Dongdaemun Shopping and Town Center Complex offers something for everyone. From materials such as burlap, faux, furs, linen, and silk, to beads and precious stones for making jewelry.

  • Textile

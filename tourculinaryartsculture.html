Culinary Arts & Culture Tour - 8days 7nights

Culinary Arts & Culture Tour - 8days 7nights

Korean food is nutritious and balanced while being low in calories. Maybe that's why it's gaining a lot of popularity among foreigners. It is widely known as a safe food that anyone can enjoy due to its unique flavor and spicy taste. On this tour, you can enjoy a variety of experiences such as making kimchi and bibimbap, royal cuisine, and tasting bulgogi, wild octopus, and makgeolli. It will be a refreshing experience to try traditional rice cakes while visiting the Rice Cake Museum in Bukchon Hanok Village!

Duration: 8 Day(s) - 7 Night(s)

Tour Category: Food Tour

caution

Korean food is rich in nutrition, well-balanced, and low in calories. It is said you can eat as much Korean food as you like and never gain weight. Widely recognized for its unique flavor and spicy taste, Korean food can easily become the safest and healthiest addiction one can enjoy for life.

1ST DAY ARRIVAL - SEOUL

Arrive at ICN airport and transfer to Seoul Hotel check-in Free at leisure & Overnight in Seoul

2ND DAY SEOUL

A.M. Visit Kimchi museum and experience Kimchi Making P.M. Visit Jongro District, the most popular area for shopping & street food

Dinner at Korea House - Enjoy Royal Cuisine traditionally prepared for kings and queens and served at
Royal Banquets accompanied by Korean Traditional Performing Arts

Overnight in Seoul

  • Kimchi

  • Jongro

  • Royal cuisine

3RD DAY ARRIVAL - SEOUL

Transfer to Jeonju.

Visit Hanok Village, one of Korea's oldest cities where the Korean Traditional LifeStyle has evolved, preserved, and maintained since AD 757 for over 1,250 years.

Activities:
- Participate in Bibimbap (Rice mixed with vegetables) & Kimchi making
- Attend Traditional Wedding Ceremony
- Visit Traditional Wine Museum
- Visit Traditional Paper Museum; experience traditional papermaking

Overnight in Jeonju

  • Makgeolli

  • Korea Traditional Wedding

  • Bibimbap

4TH DAY JEONJU - SEOUL

Transfer to Seoul

Visit Garak Agricultural and Marine Products Market, World's largest Farmer's Market, and the Noryangjin Fish Market.

Lunch: Assortment of Food Sampling at various food vendors

*Most adventurous food such as Sannakji (Wriggling Octopus) for the Daring Taste Seekers - own expense

Dinner: Makguli Tasting - Korean traditional farmer's drink that has become the national drink, with famous Korean Pancakes (Bindaeddeok made with yellow mung beans, whipped eggs and Haemulpajeon made with seafood & scallion)

Overnight in Seoul

  • Bindaeddeok

  • Garak Market

  • Haemulpajeon

5TH DAY SEOUL

Morning: City tour of Gyeongbokgung Palace, Folklore Museum, Blue House Drive-by (Presidential House), Hanok Village (Traditional Houses) near Mt. Namsan, Insadong Antique Street

Lunch: Lotte Department Restaurant Row - own expense

Afternoon: Jogyesa Temple with a tea ceremony

Dinner: Bulgogi, world-famous Korean Barbeque Beef

Overnight in Seoul

  • Gyeongbokgung

  • Folklore Museum

  • Bulgogi

6TH DAY SEOUL

Visit Bukchon Korean traditional village and Tteok museum, traditional rice cake.

Lunch: Temple Food restaurant

Visit Korean folk village & food market place

Dinner: Korean Ginseng Chicken soup with a taste of Ginseng wine

Overnight in Seoul

  • Traditional Rice Cake

  • Bukchon Traditional Village

  • Korean folk village

7TH DAY CHEONGWON - SEOUL

Visit Herb Land, a 197,700sq yard herb nursery that cultivates over 550 types of herbs. Lunch: Herb Flower Rice Return to Seoul

Overnight in Seoul

  • Herb Land

  • Herb Food

  • Herb

8TH DAY SEOUL - DEPARTURE

Transfer to Int’l Airport & Departure

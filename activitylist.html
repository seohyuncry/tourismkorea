Activity

  • 01

    N Seoul Tower & Casino Tour

    The tour begins with a visit to N Seoul Tower. Boasting 236.7 meters, the tower is so tall that it offers visitors incredible views of Seoul and the surrounding area. The view is said to offer breathtaking views of the city. You can enjoy a variety of games at Seven Luck Casino in Korea, which is considered like Las Vegas in the United States. And the shopping district located in central Gangnam is an ideal place for sightseeing and shopping.

    learn more
  • 02

    Korean Folk Village Tour

    On this tour, you can experience Korean traditional culture in detail at a place called the Korean Folk Village. Featuring a variety of residential structures, from the thatched huts of commoners to the 99-room mansions of the nobility, the buildings in the Korean Folk Village preserve Korea's rich traditional and cultural heritage. Above all, it has important meaning in that it represents the residences of commoners, farmers, and nobles throughout Korea. This is an interesting experience about the unique Korean lifestyle of the past.

    learn more
  • 03

    N Seoul Tower & Traditional Village Tour

    The N Seoul Tower & Traditional Village tour, which allows you to experience Korea's modern and past times, begins with a visit to the N Seoul Tower. Along with the N Seoul Tower, a major landmark opened to the public in 1980, go to Namsan Hanok in Namsan Traditional Village to browse Korean antiques and furniture. Afterwards, move to the streets of Insadong, where you can fully experience Korean culture, and experience galleries, traditional restaurants, traditional teahouses, and cafes.

    learn more
  • 04

    Traditional Open Market Tour

    Gyeongdong Herb Market, the starting point of the traditional market tour, supplies 70% of the raw materials for herbal medicine and is famous for having over 1,000 related stores and oriental medicine clinics. Garak-dong Agricultural Products Market is the oldest and largest seafood and agricultural products wholesale market in Korea and is located in Songpa-gu, Seoul. Lastly, visit Dongdaemun Market, which was the center of Korea's textile industry in the 1970s and is now the center of Korea's wholesale and retail fashion industry, and experience the colorful market culture.

    learn more
  • 05

    Furniture Museum, Unhyungung Palace & Jogyesa Temple Tour

    First, discover antique furniture from the Joseon Dynasty, the longest-reigning Confucian dynasty and the last to rule Korea, at the Furniture Museum. Next, you will experience Unhyeongung Palace, which was the home of the young King Gojong, one of the late Joseon emperors. Afterwards, you will visit Jogyesa Temple, which is located in the center of the city and does not have the solemn, traditional atmosphere of other temples located deep in the mountains. Afterwards, the package ends with a visit to the Insa-dong shopping district, a popular area located in the city center.

    learn more
  • 06

    Contemporary Art Gallery & National Museum Tour

    Our first stop was the National Museum of Korea, the largest museum in Korea, housing valuable cultural assets dating back to ancient times. And the Leeum Samsung Museum of Art, designed by world-famous architects Mario Botta, Jean Nouvel, and Rem Koolhaas, presents attractive buildings and works to you. Afterwards, you can see the National Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art, where you can see various classic works of modern art, and finally, feel the Samcheong-dong road that runs from Gyeongbokgung Palace to Samcheong Tunnel.

    learn more
  • 07

    Samsung Dlight & World Cup Stadium Tour

    About 40 items are on display at the Digital Pavilion, the first stop to discover fascinating IT-related products in the ubiquitous lifestyle experience center. Seoul World Cup Stadium, also known to foreigners as Sangam Stadium, was built for the 2002 FIFA World Cup. Afterwards, see cutting-edge technology at the Samsung Delight Global Exhibition Hall and feel the thrill of interacting with the items on display. At COEX, you can experience luxury hotels, aquariums, and multiplexes.

    learn more
  • 08

    Plastic Surgery Consulting & Dragon Hill Spa Tour

    Visit the street leading from Cheongdam subway station to Galleria Department Store. It is a must-visit place for local fashionistas and has become popular with Japanese tourists since it was officially designated as a fashion street. Located in the affluent district of Apgujeong-dong, Galleria Department Store plays a leading role in today's fashion industry, offering a variety of luxury brands. And visit the Samsung Delight exhibition hall, a global exhibition showcasing Samsung Electronics' latest product lineup. Finally, end your day at the oak-scented Yongsan Dragon Hill Spa.

    learn more
  • 09

    Kimchi Making & Hanbok Wearing Tour

    Take the opportunity to learn about Korea's history and culture by experiencing Korea's representative food and traditional clothing, kimchi, and Hanbok. Afterwards, visit Bukchon Hanok Village, known as a traditional village made up of Hanok (traditional Korean houses). After passing through the Samcheong-dong road tunnel and passing Samcheong Park, you will reach the final stop, Insa-dong Street. In the streets of Insa- dong, where precious and traditional goods are displayed, you can experience galleries, traditional restaurants, tea houses, and cafes.

    learn more
  • 10

    Cooking Food, Chandeokgung Palace & Namsan Hanok Village Tour

    The first stop is Cheonggyecheon, known as a haven of natural beauty located in downtown Seoul. Next, you will move to Changdeokgung Palace, the best preserved of the five royal palaces of Joseon. In this package, you will have a special experience. This is an experience where you can cook, make, and taste bulgogi yourself. Then, you will visit Namsangol Hanok Village, a traditional Korean village located between tall buildings.
